NB. Participants have been anonymized (names are fictional). The color-coded visualization of these texts is under development.

Explanation of the current color-codings:

  • Color hue indicates inter-annotator agreement for a line in the text, as computed in two ways: cosine similarity of Smooth Inverse Frequency (SIF) embeddings of the questions, or by crowdsourced human question Relatedness judgments. The latter is a lot more reliable.
    • Shades of blue: High inter-annotator agreement.
    • Shades of red: Low inter-annotator agreement.
    • Shades in between (e.g., purple): Mediocre inter-annotator agreement.
  • Color brightness on a word indicates proportion of workers who highlighted that word (out of all workers who highlighted something in that line).
    • Light/white/transparent shades: word highlighted by only small proportion of workers.
    • Pure/solid shades: word highlighted by large proportion of workers.

Possible interpretation strategy: Look for the most intensely-colored words (= cases where workers agreed on what to highlight) of the purest blue (= cases where workers' questions based on that highlight were most similar).
